Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose

Subhas Chandra Bose was an Indian Nationalist whose patriotism towards India has left a mark in the hearts of many Indians. The most well-known liberation fighter possessed exceptional leadership qualities and was a charismatic orator.

He is famously known as the founder of 'Azad Hind Fauj' and his famous slogan is 'Tum Mujhe Khoon Do, Main Tumhe Aazadi Dunga'.

Early Life

  • Born on 23 January, 1897 in Cuttack (Orissa) to Prabhavati Dutt Bose and Janakinath Bose.
  • His father was a successful lawyer in Cuttack and received the title of "Rai Bahadur".
  • He did baccalaureate from the Presidency College and was influenced by the teachings of Swami Vivekananda and Ramakrishna at the age of 16.
  • In 1920 he passed the civil service examination, but in April 1921, after hearing of the nationalist turmoils in India, he resigned his candidacy and hurried back to India.

Political Activity

  • Bose joined the Non-cooperation movement in 1921 started by Mohandas K. Gandhi, who had made the Indian National Congress a powerful nonviolent organization.
  • He started the newspaper "Swaraj" and was also the editor of the newspaper "Forward" , founded by Chittaranjan Das.
  • Bose started to work under Chittaranjan Das, a politician in Bengal. There Bose became a youth educator, journalist, and commandant of the Bengal Congress volunteers. His activities led to his imprisonment in December 1921.
  • In the year 1923, Bose was elected the President of Indian Youth Congress and also the Secretary of Bengal State Congress.
  • During the same year, Bose was arrested and sent to prison in Mandalay,Burma, where he contracted tuberculosis.
  • In 1927, after being released from prison, Bose became General secretary of the Congress party and worked with Jawaharlal Nehru for independence.
  • In late December, 1928, Bose organised the Annual Meeting of the Indian National Congress in Calcutta as General officer commanding (GOC) Congress Volunteer Corps.

Participation in Indian National Congress

  • In 1930 , he researched and wrote the first part of his book "The Indian Struggle" , which covered the country's independence movement in the years 1920-1934.
  • In 1938 he was elected president of the Indian National Congress and formed a National Planning Committee, which formulated a policy of broad industrialization.
  • On 22 June, 1939 Bose organised the All India Forward Bloc a faction within the Indian National Congress, aimed at consolidating the political left and its main strength was in his home state, West Bengal.
  • Late night on 16 January, 1941 Bose disguised as Pathan and escaped from his Elgin Road House in Calcutta, accompanied by his nephew Sisir Kumar Bose.
  • He fled to Kabul and from there, He reached Rome and finally to Nazi Germany.

Activity in Exile

  • Bose attached to a newly created Special Bureau for India, guided by Adam von Trott zu Solz that was responsible for regular broadcasts from the German-sponsored Azad Hind Radio beginning in January 1942, speaking in English, Hindi, Bengali, Tamil, Telugu, Gujarati, and Pashto.
  • A little more than a year after the Japanese invasion of Southeast Asia, Bose left Germany, traveling by German and Japanese submarines and by plane, and arrived in May 1943 in Tokyo.
  • On July 4 he assumed leadership of the Indian Independence Movement in East Asia and proceeded, with Japanese aid and influence, to form a trained army of about 40,000 troops in Japanese-occupied Southeast Asia.
  • On October 21, 1943, Bose proclaimed the establishment of a provisional independent Indian government, and his Indian National Army (Azad Hind Fauj), alongside Japanese troops, advanced to Rangoon (Yangon) and thence overland into India, reaching Indian soil on March 18, 1944.
  • INA had a separate women's unit, the Rani of Jhansi Regiment (named after Rani Lakshmi Bai) headed by Capt. Lakshmi Swaminathan, which is seen as a first of its kind in Asia.
  • Three units of INA along with japanese forces fought a stubborn battle but were defeated and forced to retreat.

Death

  • Subhas Chandra Bose's death occurred from third-degree burns on 18 August 1945 after his overloaded Japanese plane crashed in Taiwan.
